    In this groundbreaking history of Ireland, Neil Hegarty presents a fresh perspective on Ireland's past. Comprehensive and engaging, The Story of Ireland is an eye-opening account of a nation that has long been shaped by forces beyond its coasts.

    Irish history is re-examined by the Story of Ireland, questioning the established tales and long-held myths associated with Irland. Carrying readers of the past to Ireland, starting with the first settlement in A.D. 433, this is a convincing and sweeping past of one of the most diverse nations in the world. Hegarty examines how world events, including the religious wars of Europe in the 16th century, the French and American revolutions and the neutrality policy of Ireland during World War II, shaped the country over its long and fascinating history. This is an important text for readers who are intrigued by current events, politics, and history, with an up-to-date afterword that outlines the current state of affairs in Ireland.

    Spanning Irish history from its earliest inhabitants to the current financial crisis in the world, The Story of Ireland is an epic and brilliant re-telling of the history of Ireland from a new perspective.
